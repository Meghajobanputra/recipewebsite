Honey cake

Here comes an eggless Sunday Super Special. Co-Founder & Director of Lavonne Academy of Baking Science and Pastry Arts, Chef Avin Thaliath brings Eggless Honey Cake. This heavenly cake with a healthy twist will surely be loved by your whole family.

Ingredients of Honey cake:-
  1. 1/2 tin Nestlé MILKMAID
  2. 2 cups Nestlé a+ Toned Milk
  3. 1/2 cup Refined Oil
  4. 1/4 cup Melted Butter
  5. 1 tsp Vanilla Essence
  6. 1½ tsp Vinegar
  7. 2½ cups All Purpose Flour
  8. 1/2 Sugar
  9. 2 tsp Baking Powder
  10. 1 tsp Baking Soda
  11. 3 tbsp Honey
  12. 3 tbsp Mixed Fruit Jam
  13. 10 gms Desiccated Coconut

Directions:

  1. Mix and whisk all wet ingredients except milk. Then add milk and whisk again.
  2. Mix all the dry ingredients. Combine the dry ingredients with the wet ingredients. Pour the mixture inside a cake tin and bake it for 30 minutes at 180°C.
  3. For honey syrup, mix honey, sugar, and water and bring it to boil. Pour it in a bowl and let it cool down completely.
  4. For jam sauce, mix fruit jam to water, heat it, and bring to boil. Pour it in a bowl and let it cool down completely.
  5. Once the cake is completely cooled down, cut the top of the cake and make small pores using a toothpick. Pour the honey sauce and spread it with a brush.
  6. Now pour the jam sauce and spread on top of honey sauce.
  7. Garnish with desiccated coconut. Keep the cake inside the refrigerator for 10 minutes. Take it out and serve it cold.

Tips and Variations:

  1. Use high-quality ingredients, especially for the chocolate, cherries, and whipped cream, as they significantly impact the final taste of the Black Forest Cake.
  2. Accurate measurements are crucial for baking. Use measuring cups and spoons to ensure you add the right amount of each ingredient.
  3. When incorporating dry ingredients into the batter, gently fold them in using a spatula. Avoid overmixing to maintain a light and fluffy texture.
  4. Allow the cake layers to cool completely before assembling and frosting. This prevents the frosting from melting and ensures a stable structure.
  5. Use chilled cream and a cold mixing bowl to whip the cream.

Taste: Sweet and chocolaty with soft and moist texture.

Serving Ideas:

Serve it as a dessert after dinner or make it for the kid’s birthday party. Enjoy it with vanilla ice-cream or on its own.
